Email Hijacking

 

 

Nowadays, email hijacking, or email hacking, has become a common problem. Email spoofing, social engineering tools, and computer virus injection are all methods used to carry out this attack.

spoofing email

Using email spoofing, spammers send emails that seem to come from a well-known domain, causing the recipient to open the message. Typically, strange links, shaky content, and solicitations to transfer money are included in these emails.

Social Engineering

Spammers send advertisements to a variety of recipients, promising substantial savings in exchange for the recipients' personal information. In Kali, there are tools that can let you hijack an email account. Phishing tactics may also be used to attack email accounts. A link in an email may lead to the installation of malware on the user's computer or to a malicious website that asks for personal and financial information such passwords, account IDs, or credit card numbers, which can then be used to commit identity theft. Because phishing assaults are simpler to conduct than attempting to break into a computer's defenses, they are often used by cybercriminals.

Sent Viruses in a User device

The third method a hacker might use to get access to your email account is to infect your computer with a virus or other malicious program. Hackers may steal your credentials with the assistance of a virus.

How to detect email hijacking?

  • Included among the recipients of spam email are a substantial percentage of folks you know.
  • Your password no longer works.
  • You click "Forgot Password" and no email is sent.
  • Sent Items includes spam you didn't send.
