Password Cracking

Emails, databases, computer systems, servers, bank accounts, and almost everything else have passwords. Passwords unlock a system or account. People usually use easy-to-remember passwords like their birthdays, family members' names, cellphone numbers, etc. This makes passwords weak and hackable. Strong passwords protect accounts from hackers.

Strong passwords are:

  • 8-character minimum
  • Letters, numerals, and symbols
  • Mixed-case letters

 

 

Course Curriculum

Attack Types

Online Password attacks: Online password attacks are common against online apps, unprotected SSH terminals, and any login interface. An online password attack involves trying several username/password combinations to guess the correct password.

Offline Password attacks: Hashed credentials from a hacked system may be attacked offline. This is done by extracting the target system's hashes and breaking them locally. You won't scare the target if you don't associate with them.

Dictionary attacks: Dictionary attacks involve a database of semi-common passwords and hashes. A dictionary attack may match a scrambled Facebook hash to one in its database. The same may be true.

Brute-Force attacks: Brute-force approaches try every character combination. Brute-force password convert millions of passwords into hashes and compare to password hashes.

Rainbow table attacks: Rainbow table eliminates need to store millions of password-hash combinations. Rainbow tables remember recent hashes to retrieve the whole string, reducing volume and making password-hash combinations easier to find.

Social engineering: Social engineering involves obtaining sensitive information. Programmers know which emails people open carelessly. If you receive an email about a security problem with your account, don't press on an interface and input your password or other details.

Phishing: Phishing is a social engineering technique used by hackers to capture customers and steal their sensitive data for financial and information theft. Mail spoofing, URL spoofing, website spoofing, smishing, vishing, and more are types of phishing. Mail, phone, and SMS are widespread. In either of these types, the perpetrator disguises as someone from a legitimate institution and attempts to hoodwink the victims into giving sensitive information such as proof data, financial and account details, passwords, and more.

 

 

Tools

Hydra: is a dictionary attack tool. In the next example, we utilize Hydra to retrieve an FTP password.

John the Ripper: is a strong brute-force attack tool supplied with the Kali Linux distribution.

Crunch: generates wordlists using a standard or custom character set. Crunch generates all conceivable combinations. Kali Linux includes this utility.

Rainbow table: Rainbow tables store hashed passwords. It's a lookup table used to decipher passwords. Password recovery relies on a pre-calculated hash table. http://project-rainbowcrack.com/table.htm has the tables. RainbowCrack 1.6.1 uses rainbow tables. Kali has it again.
